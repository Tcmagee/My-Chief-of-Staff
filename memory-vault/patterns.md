## Approved Observations

### Builder before manager
Tommy defaults to building things himself (agents, context guides, memory
architectures) rather than delegating or requesting resources. This is a
strength when speed matters and a risk when it crowds out team development
or strategic work. Watch for: the build impulse overtaking the lead impulse.
— *Observed: March 2026 | Status: APPROVED*
### AI as identity, not just tooling
Tommy treats AI adoption as a professional identity marker, not just a
productivity tool. This drives high personal investment but also creates
risk of over-indexing on AI work at the expense of core Facilities Technology
responsibilities. The AI Champion role amplifies this.
— *Observed: March 2026 | Status: APPROVED*

### Teaches by sharing, not by instructing
When Tommy builds something useful, the instinct is to share it widely
(posted Chief of Staff agent to 3+ Slack channels within hours of feeling
it was ready). Prefers "try it and tell me what you think" over formal
training or documentation. This works for early adopters but may not
scale for the full org.
— *Observed: March 2026 | Status: APPROVED*
